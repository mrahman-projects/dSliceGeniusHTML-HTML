We have all the answers

What is an STL file, and why do I need an STL slicer? An STL file is a widely used 3D model format. To transform these models into physical objects with your 3D printer, you need an STL slicer. It converts the digital design into G-Code, the language your 3D printer understands, ensuring precise and accurate prints.
How can I customize slicing parameters with your API? Customizing slicing parameters is easy with our API. When making a slicing request, you can specify parameters like layer thickness, print speed, and infill density. These settings allow you to fine-tune the output based on your specific 3D printing needs. Set the parameter from available 800+ features.
How do I download the processed G-Code files? Downloading the processed G-Code files is a straightforward process. After your slicing request is complete, you'll receive a download link. Simply click on the provided link to access and save your G-Code file.
Where can I find the list of parameters to be included during slicing? Our slicing engine is based on CuraEngine. You can find a comprehensive list of available slicing parameters on the Cura GitHub repository  here .
What can the volume calculator API provide for my 3D designs? Our volume calculator API offers valuable insights for your 3D projects. It provides data on the total air-gap volume and overall volume, which is essential for optimizing your designs and ensuring they fit seamlessly into the real world.
How can I connect with SliceGenius on RapidAPI? Connecting with SliceGenius on RapidAPI is easy. Click the "Explore Our APIs on RapidAPI" button on our homepage, which will take you to our RapidAPI profile. From there, you can explore and integrate our APIs into your projects.
How does the pricing for your API work? Our pricing structure is designed to be flexible and accommodating. When you use our API, you get 10 free slicing operations per month for each user. If you exceed this limit, it's just $1 per slice operation for overages. For more detailed pricing information, you can visit our RapidAPI subscription page to explore the subscription tiers and options.
RapidAPI does not support attachments more than 50MB, what should I do? You can contact us at contact@3dslicegenius.com for any special requests.
